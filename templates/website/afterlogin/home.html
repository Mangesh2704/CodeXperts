Indian Food Sharing Alliance (IFSA)

The Indian Food Sharing Alliance, (IFSA) is a social initiative by the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) to help solve India’s food waste and hunger crisis by integrating various partner organizations, Food Recovery Agencies and NGO’s.

Aims and Objectives:

◉ Care: Reduce food wastage by redistributing surplus to the needy.
◉ Aware: Raise awareness about food wastage through nationwide programs.
◉ Share: Connect trained agencies with food chains for safe distribution of surplus.
◉ Prepare: Educate food businesses on best practices to prevent food loss.
◉ Declare: Support policies and programs that reduce food loss and waste.

Problem Statement

Food waste in urban areas is a growing problem, with large amounts of edible food being discarded daily due to inefficiencies in supply chains, storage, and consumption. This contributes to environmental degradation and exacerbates food insecurity. So our vision is to leverage Al to combat food waste, promoting sustainability by optimizing food distribution, reducing environmental impact, and enhancing food security in urban environments.
